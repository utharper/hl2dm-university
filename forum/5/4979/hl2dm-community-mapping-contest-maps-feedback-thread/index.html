[FF]FNDR.Jake_Brake

2010-04-20 07:52:31

Well, I obviously like pub maps as our [FF] clan runs three pub servers. First off, I agree that we have not see a real high quality rat map for a very long time, a rat map with good frames. There are just so many ideas for rat maps, where do we start; Gas Station garage, the bridge of the star ship enterprise (someone Else's suggestion), inside of a car (57 chevy?), air traffic control tower, etc.

Regardless of whether it is a rats map or not, all maps should be minimally decent performers. I personally like maps that play not only horizontally but also have a vertical aspect to them. I like maps that have a certain amount of sniping spots but they should not be too difficult to defend against. I like maps that mix equal parts of indoor areas as well as outdoor areas (Jaegar?). I do like a certain percentage of hallway gameplay but 25% or less total within the map and the hallways need to be fairly wide. The hallways aspect also allows for trip mine strategy which is good in small amounts. I do not like maps that require bunny hoping or crouch jump to get to prize areas. I like superchargers but please only one and make it easy to target. One RPG is also OK as long as it is time consuming to obtain and is also easy to target.

Weapons should be fairly easy accessible with all weapons included in the map. I like speed ladders (Haymaker). I like teleporters when they are used to increase the game flow from one side of the map to the other. I like jump pads that are strategically placed to traverse vertically 2-3 levels. I also like maps that have vantage points with long distance snipe capability, and also the ability to jump into a courtyard where there is a battle occurring.

I am not partial to kill boxes. I like and appreciate creative weapon markers and alert sounds for RPG pickup and supercharger use.

So, there you have it, my preferences. I have probably described about 3-4 different styles of maps, LOL!

CellarDweller

2010-04-20 17:30:58

league - i'm probably one of the few that doesn't mind unbalanced maps in league play. i've grown to appreciate the strategy/communication challenges to avalon, zeta or overwatch. i just wouldn't want to play unbalanced maps week after week. but for variety... its quite nice. its a bit easier to say what i don't like in league maps. clutter underfoot... gawd-awful bottles/cans/debris that slow a player down. poor clipping that impedes movement or gets players stuck. poor/dark lighting. hidden rooms. props that aren't really props that can be used. boring, mundane, indistinquishable textures (amplitute) that make player map awareness difficult. no deathtraps unless it involves high risk rpg and/or supercharger. poor spawn points... probably THE key issue with a decent league/comp map. the better clans will use and abuse spawn points w/o mercy. there has to be some serious thought put into spawn points. for example, don't put a cluster of spawns in an enclosed/semi enclosed area right next to orbs.

pub - almost anything goes with me... but there are limits. i don't mind a few hidden rooms with goodies or a death trap now and then. i prefer only one rpg and supercharger for pubs. i enjoy at least some vertical aspect to the map (at least like two story bldgs, ledges, rooftops, etc). i tend to prefer lifts over jump pads... unless you come up with a different sound for your jump pad. anything new or creative that indicates to me that the mapper spent a great deal of effort making the map. i think of maps by Valar, Pig, Boshed, Haymaker, etc. things as unique as Hanzo, or as simple and clean as Collapse. yes, there's something to be said about tradition and keeping to the traditional dystopia style of map... but creating new models, textures, colors, lighting... very sexy.

kb - not a big fan. i don't think i'd know a decent kb map if one grabbed me by the balls and squeezed.
