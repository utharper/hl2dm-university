The biggest issue with cp though is that there isnt a "round_end" like in TF2. Therefore, you have to kill all the players on the map in order to start the round over. Just check out warpath for hl2dm if you want to get a better under standing of how cp works in dm. The vmf I built is based off of it's logics and is basically identical other then the much improved capture point model.

and more...

There are not many ctf/cp maps in hl2dm bacause it's not easy to make it work well. In all they must not be more than 20-25 maps.

The only problem I see in these maps are that some of them were made from porting Team Fortress maps into hl2dm, and the mapper was noobish enough to make the weapons and items layout annalogous to TF's ammo rooms... This creates a noobish cheap-spam-fest all over them all. It's not very hard in these maps to see 8 players with RPG fighting 8 players with RPG. It's that cheap.

The maps that came next unfortunately were made following the same trend, so they aren't much better. The only map that can be considered a better map (in my honest and worthless opinion) is ctf_terminal, which has a decent weapons layout, and mimics quake flag system (can't capture the flag while enemy holds my flag).
