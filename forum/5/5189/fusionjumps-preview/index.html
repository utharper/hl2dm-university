Honestly I think you should think your maps to work with default settings. I don't see any point in training in a map if it will not be useful in practical gameplay.

I think the map is better looking and more coherent than most new jump maps I've seen coming out, but 1000 airaccelerate is plain annoying, imho.
I know that default settings are a bit limited in some ways, but people also are aware of what they can and what they can't do in default settings, since they are used to do this stuff in other maps, and this is where non-default stuff will be a bit out of context.
I'm not saying it's bad, just that it isn't very practical.

So, once again, the map looks kinda nice, like Ade said, it needs some kind of instructions in most levels, but in overall you're doing quite a nice job and it has quite a few nice ideas.

The only things I hated in this map are

- The airaccelerate setting (and if any other not default), makes it very hard to stop at small platfoms, apart from detering from a player's jumping sense.

- Level 3 bars are cylindrical. I had tried that already in a map I was making and I noticed that if you happen to land on the closest "edge" of the bar you will be proppelled upwards, and it will prevent you from going through the next bars. If you happen to land on the furthest edge you will fail to jump, probably. This makes that section suffer from inconsistency. The way I solved this in my case was to place a square player clip around them.

- Level 6 speed-crouch stuff has been proven to be a lot inconsistent in Vino's maps. It will work for some players, but will never work for others. First time I tried it I got stuck halfway into the low ceilling. Had to noclip out of it. And I'm one of the people that it doesn't work for.

- Level 7 is impossible for me with my low sensibility. Personaly I will not change sens just to be able play any map. I also think it's a bit frustrating to be sent back to beggining after doing 1st part and failing the 2nd...

I think that's it.
Anyway, good luck with it.


EDIT: Level 5 doesn't work well, I guess. I was never sent high enough to be able to bhop over the watter walls, if that's it's intention.
