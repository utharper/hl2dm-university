[EYE] Valar

OD_Knuckles wrote:About FPS:

If you see closer in some VALVe developed maps for half life 2, and deathmatch, they have lots of "Trigger" brushes and such, I believe these are for loading the rest of the map when the player goes trough a door, hallway, etc, so the FPS don't suffer, a good example is the first trainstation in Half Life 2.
kind of but not quite. the running in Single Player can andure lower FPS rates because processing and memory wise it has a lot less to do. the story is quite different in Multi Player. the whole FPS equation is a whole different story and affected by other element than just the graphics demands of the map.
For the same reason... the whole idea of areaportals and occluders which you'll find in abundance in Single Player maps doesn't work the same for Multiplayer. entities which blocks visability (of models, entities as well as whole areas) are being processed in realtime. meaning they burden the CPU and RAM all the time! even when they're not used!

Triggering parts of the map in Single Player is actually loading a new map altogether. for the player that's merely a continuance of the chapter he's in but not for the game. this of course, cannot be done in Multiplayer.

In conclusion, there is no avoiding planning out your maps with Optimization first and foremost in mind.
Check this website http://www.student.ru.nl/rvanhoorn/optimization.php
