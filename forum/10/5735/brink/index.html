For those of you feeling this game is slow or for anyone that wants it to be faster, try this:

Make an autoexec.cfg in wordpad and put the following commands in it

Seta ui_fov ## (recommend setting this to 90 or 95 in place of ##)

For dual, quad and six core processors (pretty much everyone these days)

Seta r_useThreadedRenderer 2

Put the autoexec.cfg in the steam/userdata/*yourname*/22350/local/base folder. This also where your brink mp cfg is, but they tell you not to edit that and to use an autoexec for customization.

Also use the light body type if you have a decent enough shot to survive with it. The light body type is not only faster, but has movement capability that is not available to the medium body type. As with HL2DM, keep moving!

The game is alot of fun IMO. It has alot more depth than anything we have seen in recent years. Its not run and gun COD. It requires thinking, teamwork, coordination and skill. Its not about KDR, or XP. I've seen teams with guys earning double XP compaired to the other team but losing because they didn't work as a team and do what had to be done. Its about playing smart and doing what you have to do as a team to complete the objective.

I got to say this is a text book example of how not to release a new IP. Like...

1. Do not misinform on what the game is about e.g don't say it has a in depth single player when it does not
2. Do a open beta and test it too the max. This holds true for any multiplayer based game.
3. Hire testers that know their stuff. Like emm don't hire console testers that simply don't know shit on what makes a good fps
3. Do not release a game until it is ready
4. Don't send buggy demos to reviewers
5. If it is multiplayer heavy do not allow reviewers to review the game until it is out or better yet have a open or closed beta for them.
6. If releasing on the pc platform make sure that your game in supported by both ati and nvida cards.
7. patches should address issues in this order. performance issues, game braking bugs, map balancing, ability tweaks, weapon balancing and so other stuff like stat site, dlc and other stuff that does not effect the game should be done last.

The game had a lot of potential but sadly mistakes where made which is to be expected out of a first full game released by a developer. Unlike some peeps that like to shit all over people that try and bring something new I actually try and support developer studios by taking my time to understand the game and play it my self before passing judgement. On the whole my initial reaction to the game was positive it gave me the feeling I had not had for a long time and yes I had fun and got more fun playing scrims on esl cup games than pub. But as I said in number seven major map issues on balancing where not addressed and sadly over time I grew tired off waiting for the issues to be resolved. As well they made a lot of people angry over the performance me included I got a quad and gtx560ti and I still can't get it to run smoothly. The game breaker came in the last update on gun balancing which they some how made worse by nerfing the few good guns and making it worse with random recoil which meant that it turned the game into more luck than skill.

So after all this they have sold a lot of copies so not a financial fail or success really. The game is dead on the pc and I don't think the dlc and major patch will fix it. It was a good distraction for the past fews months for me anyway and came across some new players and teams out of it.
