Walking Target - for creating a place where we can come together as a community and share our love for a little game called HL2 Deathmatch.

Valar - makes some of the most beautiful maps for the game and probably has contributed more maps than anyone else to this game.
StoneKiller - My first DM teacher and clan mate. Taught me how to play the game with class and respect for others through his example.
Zman - Always willing to help new and old nOObs alike to improve in skill and become better players, and has kindled the competative fire in me to keep going despite the beatings I take along the way.
Grape Ape - Khaos clan co-leader, without whom Khaos would never have been born or gotten as successful as it has. My confidant and person with whom I can bounce ideas off of and keeps me in check. Who never lets me forget that I once kicked his ass around a few maps and forced him to get better and can now give it back just as well as I dish out (sometimes better). And whom also reminds me ( ) that I am older than him.

So I invite everyone to think about those people in the game that are special to you in one way or another instead of whom you happen to have a low opinion of because of some past disagreement or stupidity or their part

I have found that true friends in this game are hard to come by. I don't trust people easily because I have been stabbed in the back too many times.

I will say that in the last couple of weeks I have learned who my true friends are in this game.

These are the people that despite the bumps in the road, try to work things out. They don't offer ultimatums or place conditions on your relationship. They don't use you as a stepping stone in their own ambitions or agenda. Finally they are people that are honest and straight forward rather than stringing you along with lies or vague statements.

Yes this is a game and people think they can hide behind the hollow digital image on the screen. It is a false sense of security which often causes people to let down their guard. Unless you completely refrain from any personal interaction, you can not hide your base personality. Whether you know it or not, some part of it is projected into this digital realm no matter how hard you try to stifle it. How you conduct yourself and how you relate to others says a lot about who you are behind the keyboard.
