I haven't been completely clear about what I'm thinking in this thread, so let me try to say what's in my head.

Launching a new league isn't easy. Convincing players that you're going to provide them with something worthwhile can be difficult, especially given the transient nature of most historical hl2dm leagues. Launching eFPS in North America was fairy easy, however. I don't think many North American teams felt "at home" at Clans-United and a lot of people wanted a different league. When Valiums came on board and disbanded the North American CU league, eFPS became the only real option for North American players. So getting North American players on board wasn't nearly as hard as it could have been.

The story completely changes when it comes to CU for European players, however. Clans-United has been around there for quite some time now. It's a league run by Europeans for Europeans. Their teams are used to the rules and used to how things work. It just works for them. And I respect Clans-United for providing exactly what Europeans need in a hl2dm league. Trying to take away teams from Clans-United would be incredibly difficult at this point. But more importantly - I think it would be wrong. It would be bad for the European community, bad for hl2dm relations between CU and eFPS, and just bad for everyone. Unless someone can convince me that Clans United isn't a good thing for European players and they need a new league, I just don't think its right to fracture the community between two competing options.

Let me know what you guys think.
