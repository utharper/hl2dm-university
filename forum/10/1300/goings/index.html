CellarDweller wrote:Epoch has me confused with "slacklining". I'm thinking tightlining? Yanno, fishing.
Heh yeah thats not the first time I've been confused with an angler. "Slacklining" as I refer to it is far closer to tight rope walking, but as the name implies the line is not "tight," it very elastic and wobbly while still taught enough to keep you fromm touching the ground when walking the line. Most lines are made of 1" climbing webbing, which is a flat piece of tubular webbing which is very strong (tensile strength of 4000+ lbs.) and extremely flexible. Usually when I setup a line it is about equal height with my arm pits at 50' long, and the line usually bows down like 1.5-2 feet as I walk it. Its great fun, if no one has ever seen or tried it I HIGHLY suggest it, it feels so cool. It can takes a couple days for some people to be able to stand for their first time, but once your there taking steps is far easier to learn. The equipment is cheap too.
