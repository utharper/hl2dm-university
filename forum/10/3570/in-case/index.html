To me, the singer brings some of the music down. Like when he makes every hard drum beat = a syllable (like a spondee). It makes for a manic sound, but then nothing can be emphasised because it's ALL emphasised. The singers I like tend not to be so constrained by the music, but will sing an unstressed syllable on a low beat (bass drum maybe) and stresses on the high beat (like a snare). Sometimes they skip a couple beats to work above the music say with long vowel sounds or to switch between iambs and trochees (duh-DUM .... DUM -duh). Think like Robert Plant for long vowels. Jim and many others are good for trochee/iambs.


Many times here the vocals emphasises the music and the music emphasises the voicals.
