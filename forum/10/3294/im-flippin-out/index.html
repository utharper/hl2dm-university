Team Stacking: The simple, effective answer is to enforce a roster limit in cal (and in any other leagues that spring up). 8 max for 4v4, 2 max for 2v2 (yes, 2 [two], its not hard to sync times with 1 other player and the vast majority of teams use the same 2 players in every match anyway). This would greatly increase the team count as well; instead of 20 players on a single 2v2 team, youd potentially have 10 teams of varying strengths and styles.

Lockdown: Yes, its time to let it die. A prop-choked, hugely spam-biased converted single player map designed for 16 man pubs hardly has a place in a competitive league, especially considering the numerous competition-designed maps available as alternatives. If its going to remain, for whatever reason, clip and bugfix the original and then just leave it be. The euros manage to tolerate speedcrawling, so im sure we can get used to it as well- random map specific rules are a bad idea. Speaking of which...

Lostvillage: Its time to let this die too. Numerous cheesy camp spots, free wallhax thru nodraw in various locations, props that have a 10 minute respawn timer (lol), and broken secret rooms that make new players ragequit when enemy teams camp in them, or require special rules of their own since they can be used to wait out the entire match. The maps also never going to be fixed- i think at this point its pretty certain that wolf is gone forever, and cal isnt about to start violating mappers copyright, certainly not to keep such a generic map around long after its time has passed.

Aim Arena: Deserves mention as well, for the similar reason as ld- it wasnt designed for 1v1 play, and excessively rewards aim/movement over prediction/tactics. We now have a fine selection of small 1v1 specific maps that test both sets of skills roughly equally, or allow both to be used to strong effect; let the old practice and pub maps rest and put those new competition spec maps to use.

Rates: The cal.cfg already forces rates within specific parameters. Keep in mind, someone on a bad connection is not going to want to be forced to 66/66/30000, and have to play the entire match with 100+ choke. Also, the vast majority of skipping/jittering players we see are likely on wireless, and theres not a damn thing the cal.cfg can do about that.

Draft Night: Sure, this was fun stuff (sometimes) and generally good for the community, but its not like it died because no one ever played. Someone has to run it, time / effort / commitment etc, and apparently theres no one willing to do so atm.
