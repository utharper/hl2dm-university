In Chess, you have different types of pieces: A king, the queen, bishops, knights, castles...etc.

In Starcraft, you have different types of pieces too: Tanks, zerglings, marines, big scary-ass flying ufo ships...etc.

In Chess, each type of piece has certain special abilities: A king can only move one space at a time, around it's surrounding perimeter. While the queen can move horizontally, vertically and diagonally across the entire board.

In Starcraft, each type of piece has certain special abilities: A tank can shoot from a distance, and can modulize itself to become a stationary tank; allowing it to shoot further away. While a zergling can only attack other "pieces" it is next to.

In Chess, the objective is to corner the king; called a "Check Mate."

In Starcraft, the objective is to corner the enemy and cause them to surrender, if they do not, you kill every last "piece."


I hope this sums it up for you.
