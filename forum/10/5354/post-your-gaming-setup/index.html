phantom wrote:holy fucking headset genocide how much did you pay for that
Check out the unboxing vid I made for it here: http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GVjxcGPFwuk&fmt=37

LoL, I didn't pay for the headset, but retail for it is $300. Yea I know, holy crap. I got the money from a free offer site and and used that to purchase it. Otherwise, there is no way in my right mind that I would pay 300 bucks for a headset. Thats just crazeh. But it really works, you do have to sacrifice a lot of bass to hear footsteps and I have mixed feelings for it about listening to 2.1 audio for movies and music, but overall, if you plan on going pro for a game that supports surround sound, then this headset is the way to go. Directional positioning is amazingly freaky. It works best in 1v1's 2v2's or 3v3's on large to medium size maps. I can hear if someone is behind me most of the time (if I don't its because my ears are ringing from a grenade or something) I can usually tell if someone is above or below me, its worked great in MW2, I'm only a level 13 and I was able to get 1st, 2nd, and 3rd places on some pubs using the headset. I was called a faggot hacker. Now if you go on socal server for hl2dm and you have a bout 15 noobs all around the map blaring the smg it is harder to hear exactly who is behind you or around you because the sounds are playing all around you. But it's directional positioning with having wave guides playing sounds past your front right, back right, and having 2 bass speakers, etc in the cups really helps and gives you the feeling of someone is close to you. And if you close your eyes you can literally hear where they are.

I had a friend put them on and she had never tried surround sound before so she really didn't know what was going on. So I told her to close her eyes and I then threw nades in different directions and she was able to pinpoint where they went off with her eyes closed just by listening. Without a doubt I'm glad I have this headset, I had some trouble with it the first few weeks because I had enhancements turned on with my sound card. I turned them all off and put everything at the default level and it totally changed and I felt like I was INSIDE the game. Someone shot an rpg off on a pub at me I dodged it and I FELT it go past my left ear, I almost ducked. Really freaking awesome.

If you are trying to go pro at a game, get this headset, if you are listening to music or movies and just want to experience a game, DO NOT get this headset. I recommend ONLY for people who are SERIOUS about league play. I'm still testing out this headset on a TON of games so I'll come out with a more in depth review for it in a few weeks in a video on youtube.
