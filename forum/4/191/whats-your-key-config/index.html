Read the course. It was written by me, and I scoured several clan websites looking for config info before writing it. I tried to compile the most logical config I could based on all the info I had about advanced play and reducing reaction time.

http://www.hl2dm-university.com/hl2dm_102.htm

I am happy to change it if you can give me logical reasons for why those positions are bad. M3 for use is actually very smooth. For example, you can strafe jump towards a door and hit use to open it all without taking your fingers off any of your movement keys. You also have full access to all movement keys while charging. If you use default "E" for use, you cannot strafe right without moving your finger back first. The only reason I can think of for not putting it on M3 is that you might want to use your mouse wheel for some other purpose.

1 is not so far away when it's a direct bind with fastswitch on. How many pistol kills are you getting in a real match anyway?

The course recommends slot 1 with fast switch for melee, which Jelly likely has. Why not use W if it's free and your movement keys are ESDF? Melee is one of the weapons you absolutely need to be able to draw quickly if you are going to use it at all. The course assumes you have either a standard mouse, or a mouse with only the extra thumb button. If you have more you can put it on your mouse, just bear in mind that if you ever play in a competition where you are not allowed to bind those extra buttons, you might be screwed. This config is written with that in mind.

A note about key setups.

I have changed mine multiple times. It really is not as hard as people make it out to be, I would do it after the end of a season or the beginning of a season rather then before playoffs but you can do it and it is worth it. I suggest not being afraid to move your config around for other reasons. For one it teaches you a skill, how to learn something, some of your decade old gamers who have never moved your config have also not learned to learn in a decade either. You do things 100% without thinking or trying because you have done it so long and in fact you may have never learned how to increase your skill at something quickly. Second I have seen players who are real good like conflict change their config around and become a monster like LukeGT421. The point was that for a long time they were hitting a glass ceiling based on their config. And getting things set up right raised the bar and unleashed them to achieve their full potential. It did take about a month for them to get back to where they were, but in an extra month they were well past that.

Anyone can be pretty good at the game with literally any setup. The problem is they will have weakness in certain situations because they either cannot do some key stroke, it takes them longer, or it inhibits them from doing something else while they are doing it.

Here is an example: When I am charging I must move my index finger off of right strafe and so I cannot strafe left and right to minimize the chance of a headshot from someone who heard me charging. So I work around this by moving my middle finger down to right strafe. Then when I am done or interrupted I need to replace my fingers to the right keys. If I am not in good practice I will mess this up and usually get killed. Perhaps end up on the wrong keys or just not move quick enough. This is probably not worth moving my keyboard config around for but for other people something much more important could be inhibited. Something they may need to do constantly.

The problem with the Arrow keys is that they have a whole bunch of dead space and large keys near them. This means that you are limited in the number of keys you can press quickly. Currently I run an ESDF config, previously I ran an RDFG config but due to crap keyboard problems I had to move closer to the alt keys. The main point is if you have more keys with in quick reaching distance of your finger you will be able to do more tasks with binds and do them quicker. Perhaps some time later I will write up a more in depth look at how to design a keyboard layout and why you would place things in some area.

Dell actually makes pretty good free keyboards and kotao speaks the truth I have no problem spending money on things but the gaming keyboards have yet to sell me.

Now lets clarify. Not all keyboards are made the same so particular key combos may work for one person and not the other. Most people do not even realize they are the victim of this keyboard flaw since the extra keys they want to press are weapon keys and they usually just think it was user error. So you may want to test your keyboard out if you notice that you thought you pressed something but nothing happened. Some people just do not hit enough keys fast enough to notice and many do not even bunny hop.

The reason that companies do not fix this problem is that most people do not need to press lots of keys all at the same time even in most games. For some reason valve always makes their games with tons of unintended exploits or advanced techniques which lead to the need to hold down various keys at the same time. If you think about what you need to do in an average game of HL2DM without scripts you must dedicate a finger to 6 keys. So the chances you are going to be hitting some combo that won't work are higher then other games where it's mostly just movement and jump. There are some groups of people who notice this problem though if you search you will find that blind people actually suffer they have to hit combinations of sdf and jkl to write braille and cannot do it on certain keyboards.

One test that can get you a better keyboard is this. Pick the keys you plan to hit all the time and put fingers on them all. Then press them all down at once, repeat 4 times and press enter. Do that process 10 times. If your keyboard is capable you should have all the letters in each row forming perfect rectangle but the order of them will be somewhat random. Like this:
