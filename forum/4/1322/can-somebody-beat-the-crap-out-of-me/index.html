It's most likely like most killbox players that your sensitivity is too low. In a killbox you nearly always have very large distances between yourself and your opponent so you would desire a lower sensitivity or at least alter the way you aim to deal with that large distance. In a smaller map like lockdown you need quick reflexes in short distances unlike killbox a guy surprising you around the corner is common. If your aim is really good it won't matter if it is splash damage or orbs or mags or shotguns they all kill pretty fast. The only exception is the xbow since it has no head shot damage in a 1v1 it is not as usefull for direct hits unless you are really good with it. Cause the guy is going to have armor and basically absorb the first hit. The more players present the stronger the xbow becomes as they have less chance to be stocked with armor.

Knowing the map inside and out does give you an advantage but that is true of all maps, anytime I drop into a killbox it is rough to start but by the end of the map I usually have found enough mags and armor to turn the game around.

Yes if your movement sucks a player can basically toy with you he can come in with 100/200, shoot you several times and if you get him low he can just run away and you aren't fast enough to catch him and finish him. Then he has plenty of time to run around and restock and come back and try again.

Not sure about this jittery movement you are talking about in LD R3

Each "normal DM" (I don't like to call the non-killbox maps stock cause some people mistake that for default, non-custom) map has different weapons to whore, I guess. For Lostarena, I find the two SMG nades key with Magnum being the second most important weapon. In Lockdown, it's definitely orbs and nades. For Overwatch, the RPG, Magnum, Crossbow, and SMG Nades are all useful, it's just a matter of where and when you get them to start dominating the map with ease. I find that in most killboxes, the weapons are laid out and generally easy to get, providing a more even-sided battle at all times rather than a weapon/item-resource controlled one. With the killbox's sheer size, you really want to get good at sniping.
